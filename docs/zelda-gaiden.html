Zelda Gaiden and Majora's Mask differences

Expect more stuff to be added sometime... maybe.
Zelda: Gaiden was what the project name of The Legend of Zelda: Majora's Mask was called. I am pretty interested in it, so at this page I will talk about various differences and my opinions about Zelda Gaiden and its final product.

Story
The story of Zelda: Gaiden was even in some earlier stages probably not going to be much different than that of the final version. Of course, since it first was developed for the N64DD, a lot has probably been cut out of the game, but there is nothing such that we know about to date, except that the game would have progressed in real time and set over a week rather than three days.
According to IGN's old previews of ZG it seems like some differences in the intro for example were that Skullkid was named "Stalkid" (may just have been a mistype by them, though) and that this "Stalkid" tells Link that he has stolen Epona rather than doing it right in front of him (that may be false, since the trailer of ZG shows the cutscene from the final game). The biggest difference is probably that Navi was in the game instead of Tatl, as shown in some screenshots (this may just have been a place holder though). According to IGN (again) there was also going to be a "dark" version of Navi in ZG, which may refer to either Tael or Tatl. I am not sure why Link would walk around in the Lost Woods if he had Navi with him, though...
There is also a quite known interview with Miyamoto where he stated that there would be a "Jabu-Jabu fishing game" which could "prevent the moon from falling". There doesn't seem to be any traces of this left in the game, and to me it all pretty much seems like bullshit. Yes, it was said by Miyamoto himself, but it is a fact that he wasn't very involved in the development of MM.

  • Screenshot showing Navi in Zelda: Gaiden

Gameplay
The biggest difference with the gameplay between ZG and MM is probably that it originally was going to have seven days instead of three, and that the game would progress in real-time. In an interview Miyamoto said that it there was a merchant who was "selling time", that may indicate that the Song of Time was not originally going to warp Link back in time and that the player had to buy time to not be crushed by the moon.

Items
The items in Zelda Gaiden seems to have gone through rather big changes. Here are most of the unused ones:
Adult's Mask
The most popular unused item from ZG is probably the "Adult's mask", an unused mask which probably was used to turn Link into adult Link. It can be discussed what function adult Link was going to have in the game, since young Link now could use most of the items that he could not in OoT. I think that adult Link just was going to work as a stronger version of young Link (much as Fierce Deity Link), using the Biggoron's sword and thus dealing more damage. I think so mostly due to the facts that the model of Biggoron's sword still is in the final game (it is probably a leftover from OoT, though) and that all the transformations of Link was (and is) using an item or function which young Link could not use, and then adult Link would probably also have used another weapon or something. He could also have been using one handed weapons and a shield, but I cannot find any reasons for why he would do that. Another theory I have is that he actually would have the same function as in OoT, being able to use certain items that young Link could not for example.
  • The "adult's mask"

Link Mask
The purpose of this mask is largely unknown, and the only traces left of it in the game is display lists in Skull Kid's file. It can be assumed that this was used to turn Link back to his hylian form when being transformed  with another mask, however I don't think that this is the case. There are three display lists of Majora's mask in Skull Kid's file, each rotated differently for certain scenarios. There are also three display lists of this "Link Mask", each one of these rotated exactly like the other three MM display lists. Thus it is very possible that Skull Kid actually would have worn the mask at some point in development. The purpose of this can be discussed, but similar to Super Mario Sunshine he may have used the mask to impersonate Link and make people in Clock Town dislike him or something.
  • The "child's mask", as shown using a display list viewer

Deku Pipes, Goron Drums and Zora Guitar

Fairy Ocarina
In MM Link was apparently originally going to use the fairy ocarina instead of the ocarina of time, as seen in many screenshots. I don't know why he would want to use the crappy fairy ocarina instead of oot, since the fairy ocarina could not let him warp in time. Yes, he hands over the ocarina of time to Zelda in the end of OoT, but then he warps back to being a kid when he still owned the ocarina of time. It is also possible that the fairy ocarina was used while the ocarina of time was stolen, and that the story had a bit more with retrieving the ocarina from skull kid or something like that. Yet another possibility is that the Song of Time originally wasn't even present in MM, or atleast didn't have the same function.The icon of the fairy ocarina is still left in the final product, but when used it acts like the pictobox.

Megaton Hammer
This hammer that was used in OoT was probably going to be used by Goron Link as seen in screenshots from earlier stages of MM. It was probably replaced by the punching and ground pounding thing he can do. I think that it would have been cooler to have the Megaton hammer instead of the things that were used in the final game, but that is just my opinion.
